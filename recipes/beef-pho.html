Beef Pho

What is Pho?

Pho (pronounced "fuh") is a light, fragrant noodle soup eaten for breakfast and all through the day. Traditionally, it contains thin, sliced rare beef, which is cooked in the bowl by the fragrant broth.
It is one of the Vietnam's most loved dishes.

Ingredients

Stock

  • 2 onions
  • 10cm piece of ginger
  • 21/2 beef soup bones
  • whole chicken (boiled)
  • 5 star anise
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 8cm piece of cassia bark
  • 450g beef brisket or chuck steak
  • 11/2 tbsp salt
  • 80ml (1/3 cup) fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar

For Bowls

  • 1 kg dried or fresh pho noodles
  • 225g beef sirloin, finely sliced across the grain
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 4 spring onions (scallion), green part only, finely sliced
  • 1/3 cup chopped coriander
  • black pepper

Garnishes

  • lime wedges
  • sliced chillies
  • Vietnamese mint sprigs
  • bean sprouts

Steps

Cooking

  1. To prepare the stock, sear the onions and ginger over a naked flame or under a grill for about 15 minutes. Remove any charred skin and set aside.

  2. Place the bones in a large pot and cover with cold water. Bring to the boil and cook vigorously for 3 minutes. Discard the water and rinse the bones in warm water.

  3. Wipe out the pot, return the bones to it and add 6 litres water. Bring to the boil then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer. Skim off any scum.

  4. Add the onion, ginger and remaining ingredients and cook for 1½ hours.

  5. When the meat is cooked (slightly chewy but not tough), remove it and set aside in a bowl of cold water for 10 minutes. Remove from the water and refrigerate.

  6. Leave the broth to cook for a further 1½ hours. Strain and refrigerate. Skim off the fat when cold.

Serving

  1. To serve, reheat the stock. Thinly slice the cold meat. If using dried noodles, soak in hot water for 15-20 minutes, until soft. If using fresh noodles, briefly heat them in boiling water.

  2. Arrange the noodles in the bases of deep serving bowls and add slices of cooked meat and raw sirloin on top. Garnish with onion, spring onion and coriander. Season with pepper. Ladle over the hot stock.

  3. To eat pho, taste the broth first, then add lime juice and chilli to taste followed by generous amounts of mint and beansprouts.
